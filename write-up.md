INTRODUCTION
For this assignment, I designed a deterministic Daily Reflection Tree that guides users through an end-of-day reflection process using fixed questions, predefined options, and branching logic. The system does not use any LLM at runtime and ensures a consistent experience.

Why I Chose These Questions

I selected simple and practical questions that users can answer easily after a workday. The aim was to make the reflection feel natural, not like a test.

Examples:

“How was your day overall?” opens the session simply.
“What helped most today?” explores ownership.
“How did you contribute today?” shifts focus to value creation.
“Who was on your mind most today?” expands awareness beyond self.
Branching Logic Design

The tree follows three axes in order:

Locus of Control – ownership vs external blame
Orientation – contribution vs entitlement
Radius of Concern – self-focus vs broader awareness

Each answer routes the user to a different follow-up question or reflection, making the experience structured and personalized.

Why Deterministic Design

A deterministic system ensures that the same answers always lead to the same output. This makes the tool predictable, auditable, and easy to improve.
